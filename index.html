The Problem

Spreadsheets were never built for scheduling, and there are few other options

We've written more about this: The Scheduler the System Forgot

One sheet, total overload

Cram every section onto a single sheet and it becomes a wall of data. Finding what you need means scrolling, squinting, and hoping you don't miss a conflict.

Many sheets, no coordination

Split by instructor or program and things look cleaner — until you change a room in one tab and forget to update two others. Sheets don't talk to each other readily. If you've got the time, adding macros and formulas can paper over some of these problems, but they tend to be brittle, and the breaks are permanent when the person who wrote them moves on.

Errors you find too late

Double-booked rooms, overloaded instructors, time-slot collisions — in a spreadsheet, these stay invisible until someone complains or the registrar catches them. Then it's an afternoon of panicked emails...

Big systems, no real help

Centralized timetabling platforms collect everyone's constraints and hand back a schedule — but they were never built to help you shape it. Outside that black box, there's little offered for the person who actually makes the decisions. Our comparison page will help you compare systems and alternatives.

TermPoint fills the gap.

What is TermPoint?

TermPoint is a desktop course-timetabling application for university and college departments. It is a visualization and record-keeping tool, designed with the end-user in mind. Unlike large centralized systems, TermPoint assumes you will make the best decisions about your schedule, if only you could see it clearly. It runs locally on the user's desktop without a need for databases, cloud-software and registrar infrastructure.

01

Filter to what matters right now

View your schedule by instructor, by room, by day, by program — or any combination. TermPoint shows you exactly the slice you need and hides the clutter. Switch views instantly without losing context.

02

One change, every view updated

Move a section to a different time and the room view, instructor view, program view all update together. No cross-referencing tabs. No copy-paste errors. One source of truth.

03

Conflicts surface, not hide

Room collisions, instructor overlaps, and student-access issues are flagged the moment they happen — not after the schedule goes to the registrar. Potential problems are brought to your attention; you still make the decisions.

Installation

TermPoint is a self-contained desktop application — no .NET runtime, database server, or other infrastructure is required. Just download, run, and start scheduling.

Network / Shared Use (optional)

For teams that share a schedule, TermPoint can read and write a single database file on a shared network folder. No special server software is needed — standard Windows file-sharing permissions are sufficient.
